We are all children of our beloved Heavenly Father and the Lord whom we must look up to.

Thank you for allowing us to feel that truth once again through this beautiful song, through our beloved United Single Adults Choir.

It was such a beautiful song. Thank you.

Last weekend, my husband and I had the opportunity to attend a stake conference in Tokyo we also had the opportunity to visit three sisters and their families.

coincidentally, all three sisters were terminally ill. But, while visiting, I saw that the sisters understood Heavenly Father’s perfect plan of salvation and endured this hardship with faith in power, so I was so touched to see them so joyfully.

one of the sisters had cancer cells that had spread throughout her body, and the doctor said that there was nothing more she could do, so she had only about two months to live.

So we visited her. So now, I think she was so tired that she couldn’t even come to see us. I was worried about what to do if it was hard, so I went in the door, but that sister was smiling so brightly and saying that it was dinner time in the kitchen, so she was diligently and happily preparing food for us.

As I looked at the sister, she said that of course she was physically and mentally tired, but she was thinking about how she could serve and help others in this situation.

She said that her husband was in charge of the missionary work, and that her husband helped her diligently, and that two baptisms had come out recently.

She said that she was so happy because she was living in joy, and she felt that her body was becoming more energetic and better. When I saw that sister, who was really struggling, did not get frustrated by her own difficulties, but instead reached out to others,

I thought that she was a person who really resembled the Lord. At the general conference last October, I would like to share a word that Elder Quentin Cook said.

It was one of my favorite tables. It was about following Christ in peace. He spoke about where it is possible to be, and he said this: We do not know all the answers to what happens in life, but we do know important principles that will help us face realization, hardship, and adversity with faith and confidence in the bright future that awaits each of us.

I testify that those who follow Christ in peace will find personal peace both in this life and in our glorious reunion in heaven. I was strengthened by the words of the State of the Union.

I testify that even in this climate, when we look to Jesus Christ and focus on the peace and healing He offers, all the difficulties and hardships we face can be transformed into joy and hope. I testify that our Heavenly Father knows and loves us all, and that Jesus is my Redeemer and Savior. In the name of Jesus Christ, amen.
